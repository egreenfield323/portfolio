DNS Directors

As a part of an Intro to Game Design course I took, I made a board game called "DNS Directors".

Here is a link to the downloadable print-n-play pdf of my game. The PDF contains the rules, and supplies needed to play the game.

Warning, the pdf might take a while to load.

For a general rundown:

Overview

Become an engineer of the internet and race to send clients to their requested website before your opponents do!

Setup
  • Shuffle the deck of cable cards and lay them out to form the starting array of cables.
    • 2 players → 3x3
    • 3-4 players → 4x4
    The remaining cable cards go into a side deck.
  • Shuffle the destination cards and place three of them on each side of the cable array.
  • Take the request cards and make four decks for each side.
    • The three request card decks should correspond to the destination cards of each side.
    • Ex: A side with destinations Youtube, Bing, and Google would have Youtube, Bing, and Google in their request cards deck.
Gameplay

At the beginning of the game, every player draws one card from everybody’s request deck. These decks should have the same number of cards as the number of players, minus one. These are the requests that players must complete.

A player has these options when it is their turn.

  • Draw a cable card if they have less than 3 in their hand (free).
  • Rotate a cable card.
  • Play a new cable card.
  • Begin sending a signal (free use. Does not take a full turn).
